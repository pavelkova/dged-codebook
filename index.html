Forced Displacement
This can include forced evictions, religious persecution, or ethnic cleansing, as well as concerted attempts to make the ethnic group move even if this is not due directly to the ethnic identity of the group.
Exclusions: Do not code if the only reason for moving is because the group is nomadic or because the group is moving for economic reasons unrelated to their ethnic membership.


Genocide

Genocide: Deliberate killing of a large group of people based on nationality or ethnicity.

This can include:
  1. State-sponsored killings that are ostensibly for political reasons but that differentially affect some ethnic groups over others.
  2. Political violence where deaths are concentrated within an ethnic group.
  3. Mass killings that uniquely affect a large number of members of a specific ethnic group.
  4. “Tribal conflict” where one ethnic group is subjected to lethal violence from another.
  5. Targeted killings based on land disputes and in response to rebellions.

Please record in the comments, the approximate number of individuals killed.

Ethnocide

Ethnocide: deliberate and systematic destruction of the culture of an ethnic group (including their language, religion, or way of life)

This can include:
  1. Punishing members of an ethnic group for engaging in cultural, religious, or linguistic practices characteristic of the ethnic group from any kind of party (e.g., not just the government).
  2. State policies that restrict teaching of an ethnic group’s language, religion, or cultural practices.
  3. An ethnic group having their traditional means of livelihood taken away due to land grabs or destruction of their environment.
  4. Government efforts to change cultural practices even if these are objectionable to the coder.
  5. Governments’ failure to recognize the existence of an ethnic group.
  6. Documented microaggressions aimed at an ethnic group’s cultural, religious, or linguistic practices.
This can include state policies that restrict the practice of cultural, religious, and linguistic practices of all non-majority groups.
EXCLUSIONS: Simply being forced from land, experiencing repression, or being deprived of education and healthcare without clear evidence that it systematically destroyed the culture, way of life, religion or language of the group should not be counted here. Language loss that is not a result of a concerted effort should be excluded. Exclude simply mention of laws restricting immigration of members of this group.


Forced Labor and Slavery
Forced labor means that people have no choice as to whether they do work for or offer services to another. This can be due to threat of physical violence or laws or informal institutions tying people to an owner, a relationship or a tract of land. This can include:
  1. Forced labor due to outsiders colonizing the specific country.
  2. Members of the ethnic group targeted for capture to be sold as slaves in other countries.
This can also include situations where members of the ethnic group would be punished, persecuted, or left with no outside option if they didn’t pursue the work that society demands of them.
  • If it is not clear whether this happened within the borders of the country, then code as Y and make a comment.
  • If members of this ethnic group were systematically enslaved by members of a specific other ethnic group, please record the other ethnic group in the comment section.
EXCLUSIONS: This should not include cases where members of the ethnic group were enslaved by others within the same ethnic group (see SLAVECASTE for this).




If they enslaved members of a specific other ethnic group, please record this in the comment for this section.


If they enslaved members of a specific other ethnic group, please record this in the comment for this section (and also the time period that this occurred during).

This can include so-called feudal systems where some members of an ethnicity were subjected to long-term forced labor without compensation by other members of the ethnicity.

Privilege

Ethnic Recognition

In only rare cases are entire ethnic groups deprived of citizenship in a country, but we record this for those rare cases.

(e.g. Myanmar does not recognize Rohingya as an official nationality, Roma are not officially recognized in many eastern European countries).
This should be coded as Y, unless there is specific evidence that members of the group are not recognized as citizens. Simply mention of “secondary citizen” still counts as a Y, because they are still citizens unless additional evidence is provided.


Language

These codes will give information about the status of the ethnic group’s primary language and the privilege experienced by an ethnic group by having the state language as their primary language.

This only counts for languages that are designated for use in official state affairs and used in official state documents. What counts as a distinct language (e.g., Serbian vs. Croatian) will depend on what are the most salient distinctions in the specific country.
  • If the ethnic group has shifted to using the state language in recorded history, code Y and leave a comment.
  • If the group doesn’t yet almost universally use this language in the home, then code N.

In the comment box, please indicate the State language(s) as well as the primary language of the ethnic group.

Religion

This provides information about whether the religion of the ethnic group is the privileged religion in a country.

The dominant religion means the religion practiced by the most people. What counts as a distinct religion (e.g., Protestant vs. Catholic) will depend on what are the most salient distinctions in the specific country. This should count as Y even if the religion is a mixed religion (see below). If the ethnic group has shifted to primarily this religion in recorded history, code Y and leave a comment.

In the comment box, please indicate the country’s dominant religion as well as the dominant religion of the ethnic group.

Is the currently dominant religion of the ethnic group in this country a mixture of traditional beliefs/practices and the dominant religion in the country? This means the religion is a mixture of practices and influences and not simply that some people practice one religion and others practice another. Both RELIGION and RELIG_MIX can be Y if applicable.

Colonization

Colonization: A foreign ethnic group establishing a government in a new region that exerts social control over the existing population and resources of a region. Although colonization might involve migration of members of the foreign ethnic group to the new region, migration is not sufficient to count as colonization. Territorial invasions during wars (e.g., German takeover of European countries during WW2) as well as administrative dominance (e.g., Soviet control over satellite states in Eastern Europe and Mongolia) count here.

This also counts if the ethnic group gained a powerful position in society because it was an ally of the colonizer. See definition of colonization above.
  • If the ethnic group is a mixture of past colonizers and colonized populations, then put Y for both COLONIZED and COLONIZER.



See definition of colonization above.
